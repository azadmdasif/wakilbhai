## What stamp duty is and when you pay it

Stamp duty is a state tax paid on legal documents — rent agreements, sale deeds, gift deeds, mortgages and more. Paying the correct duty is what makes the document fully usable: an under-stamped agreement can be refused as evidence in court and attracts penalties of several times the shortfall when it surfaces. Because stamp duty is a **state subject**, the rate for the same document can differ sharply between, say, Delhi and Maharashtra, and states revise their schedules regularly.

This calculator gives an **indicative estimate** for the three documents people ask about most:

- **Rent agreement** — duty is usually a small percentage of the annual rent, or of the total rent plus deposit for the term. Many states cap the amount for short residential agreements, which is why the common 11-month agreement is cheap to stamp.
- **Sale deed** — duty is a percentage of the higher of the sale price or the government circle rate. This is the big-ticket item: on a ₹50 lakh flat, a 6% duty is ₹3 lakh, before registration fees.
- **Gift deed** — many states charge concessional or token duty when property is gifted to close family members, and full sale-deed-like duty otherwise.

Several states also give a **rebate to women buyers**, and most add a registration fee (commonly 1%) on top of the duty shown here.
