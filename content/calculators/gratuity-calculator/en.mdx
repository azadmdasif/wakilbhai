## How gratuity works in India

Gratuity is a statutory lump-sum benefit under the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, paid by an employer when an employee leaves after long service — on resignation, retirement, retrenchment, or to the family on death. It applies to establishments with **10 or more employees**, and once the Act applies, it continues to apply even if headcount later falls.

The eligibility rule is what trips most people up: you generally need **5 years of continuous service** with the same employer. The 5-year condition does not apply when employment ends due to death or disablement. Some High Courts have accepted **4 years and 240 days** as sufficient, but that position varies — treat 5 years as the safe rule and take advice in borderline cases.

The formula itself is simple:

**Gratuity = (15 ÷ 26) × last drawn monthly salary × completed years of service**

Three details matter. "Salary" means **basic pay plus dearness allowance** — not your full CTC, so the number is usually smaller than people expect. Service of **more than 6 months in the final year rounds up** to a full year (7 years 7 months counts as 8). And the total payout is capped at the statutory maximum, currently **₹20 lakh** for employees covered by the Act.

## If your employer doesn't pay

Gratuity is due within **30 days** of it becoming payable; beyond that, interest applies. If an employer delays or refuses, you can apply to the **Controlling Authority** under the Act (usually the local labour office) — a low-cost, lawyer-optional forum that can order payment with interest. A formal demand notice first, itemising the computation from this calculator, resolves many cases without any proceedings.
