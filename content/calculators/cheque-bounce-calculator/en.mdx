## Why the dates matter more than anything else

A cheque bounce case under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act is won or lost on the calendar. The law fixes three windows, and each starts when the previous event happens: you must send a written demand notice within **30 days** of receiving the bank's return memo; the cheque issuer then has **15 days** from receiving the notice to pay; and if they don't, the criminal complaint must be filed within **one month** of that window closing. Add the underlying rule that a cheque is only valid for **3 months** from its date, and you have four deadlines stacked on each other.

This calculator lays those dates out for you. Enter the cheque amount, the date on the return memo (or the date you received it), and the date you sent or plan to send the legal notice — it shows the last safe date for each step. It also shows what is financially at stake: on conviction the fine can extend to **twice the cheque amount**, and courts can order **interim compensation of up to 20%** of the cheque amount while the trial is pending.
