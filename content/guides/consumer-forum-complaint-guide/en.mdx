## When a consumer complaint applies

The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 covers you when you bought **goods or services for consideration** and suffered from:

- **Defective goods** — a faulty phone, appliance, vehicle, adulterated food
- **Deficiency in service** — banking, insurance, telecom, travel, coaching, hospitals, builders, repair services
- **Unfair or restrictive trade practices** — false advertising, warranty denial, one-sided terms
- **Overcharging** or sale of goods above MRP, unsafe goods

E-commerce purchases are squarely covered, and the platform itself owes grievance redressal duties. What the Act does **not** cover: goods bought for resale or purely commercial purposes, free services, and contracts of personal service.

## The three-tier forum system

| Forum | Claim value (goods/services paid + compensation sought) | Where |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **District Commission** | Up to ₹50 lakh | Every district |
| **State Commission** | ₹50 lakh – ₹2 crore | State capital (plus benches) |
| **National Commission (NCDRC)** | Above ₹2 crore | New Delhi |

{/* VERIFY: pecuniary limits per the 2021 revision notification — District ≤ ₹50 lakh, State ≤ ₹2 crore, National above */}

Territorially, the 2019 Act made life easier: you can file **where you reside or work**, not only where the seller is — which matters for online purchases.

**Limitation:** 2 years from the cause of action. **Appeals:** District → State within 45 days; State → National; National → Supreme Court.

## Step 1 — Complain to the seller first (and keep proof)

Commissions expect to see that you gave the seller a chance:

1. Use the seller's or platform's **grievance channel** and note the ticket numbers.
2. Send a **written complaint letter** (our free format is linked below) stating the purchase details, the problem, what you want (refund/replacement/repair/compensation), and a deadline of 15 days.
3. If ignored, escalate with a **legal notice through an advocate** — not mandatory for consumer cases, but it forces a decision and often produces the refund without any case being filed.

Also try the **National Consumer Helpline (1915 / consumerhelpline.gov.in)** — it mediates with companies and many complaints resolve there free of cost.

## Step 2 — File on e-Daakhil

If the demand fails, file the complaint — no lawyer needed:

1. Register on **edaakhil.nic.in**, the online filing portal of the consumer commissions.
2. Upload the complaint (facts in simple language, relief claimed), an **affidavit**, and your **evidence**: invoice, warranty card, photos, emails, chat records, the complaint letter/notice and the seller's replies.
3. Pay the fee online. Fees are slab-based and light — currently **nil for claims up to ₹5 lakh**, and a few hundred to a few thousand rupees on higher slabs. {/* VERIFY: current fee slabs under the 2020 rules */}
4. The Commission scrutinises and admits the complaint, then issues **notice to the opposite party**, which must reply (normally within 30 days).
5. Hearings follow — many commissions allow **video-conference appearance**. Mediation may be offered; it is voluntary.

## What you can claim

Frame the relief concretely:

- **Refund** with interest, or **replacement**, or free **repair**
- **Compensation** for loss, injury, mental agony and harassment — keep it proportionate and justified
- **Litigation costs**
- Orders to **stop the unfair practice** or withdraw misleading ads

## Practical tips that move cases

- **Sue the right parties.** For online orders, name the platform and the seller; for builder cases, the builder entity on the agreement.
- **Quantify everything.** A specific claim ("refund ₹42,500 with 9% interest, ₹10,000 costs") is easier to grant than "suitable compensation".
- **Keep originals** of invoices and correspondence; upload clean scans.
- **Small claims are viable** — the zero fee up to ₹5 lakh and in-person appearance make even ₹5,000 disputes worth filing when the principle matters.

If the opposite party ignores a final order, execution proceedings can attach property or trigger imprisonment — orders of consumer commissions have real teeth.
